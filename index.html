Olympic Swimmer

Katie Ledecky, by name of Kathleen Genevieve Ledecky, (born March 17, 1997, Washington, D.C., U.S.), American swimmer who was one of the sports dominant freestylers in the early 21st century, breaking numerous records.

Ledecky made her first splash in international swimming when she set an Olympic-trials record in the 800-metre freestyle to qualify for the 2012 London Games as the youngest member of the U.S. Olympic swimming team. She won 10 Olympic medals, 7 of which were gold.

She set a global standard in the 400-metre freestyle with a time of 3 minutes 58.86 seconds, thus becoming the first female swimmer since American Janet Evans to hold simultaneous world records for the 400-, 800-, and 1,500-metre distances.
